# The Story of West African Music — Installment 7: Modern Afrobeats & the Global Takeover (c. 2000-present)

The story closes where West Africa now leads global pop. After Nigeria's 1999 return to democracy, cheap digital studios and a new industry (Kennis Music and the rest) rebuilt Lagos pop from scratch; 2Baba's 2004 ballad 'African Queen' announced a modern Afropop, and P-Square and D'banj carried it across the continent. Then, in the 2010s, AFROBEATS (with an 's' -- the polished, log-drum-and-synth Lagos/Accra pop, distinct from Fela's Afrobeat) went global: Wizkid, Davido, and Burna Boy filled arenas from London to New York, Burna took home a Grammy, and by the 2020s Rema's 'Calm Down', CKay's 'Love Nwantiti', and Wizkid & Tems' 'Essence' were among the biggest songs on Earth. Ghana's hiplife and azonto ran in parallel. The cross-links are now a two-way flood: Drake and Beyonce sing over Afrobeats, and South African AMAPIANO (the next survey) reshapes the sound. And it all traces home -- Burna Boy is the grandson of Fela's manager and samples him directly, closing the loop back to Installment 5. Region: artists are Nigerian and Ghanaian; diaspora and foreign collaborators (Beyonce, Drake, and UK-based artists) are flagged, with the West African lead credited.

## THE DIGITAL DAWN: 2BABA, KENNIS MUSIC & THE 2000s REBIRTH (c. 2000-2008)

Nigeria's new democracy and cheap home studios sparked a pop rebirth. Out of the R&B group Plantashun Boiz came 2Baba (2Face Idibia), whose 2004 'African Queen' is the fountainhead of modern Afropop; galala star Daddy Showkey brought the ghetto sound, and Eedris Abdulkareem's 'Jaga Jaga' brought protest. This is the industry rebuilding itself from zero.

- Only Me — 2Baba (2Face Idibia)
- Diana — Daddy Showkey
- Jaga Jaga — Eedris Abdulkareem
- Olufunmi — Styl-Plus
- Yori Yori — Bracket

## P-SQUARE, D'BANJ & THE MO'HITS TAKEOVER (c. 2007-2012)

The late 2000s belonged to the twins P-Square and to the Mo'Hits camp of D'banj and producer Don Jazzy. D'banj's 2011 'Oliver Twist' cracked the UK Top 10 -- the first real sign Afropop could go global -- while 9ice's Yoruba-proverb hip-hop and Wande Coal's silky vocals set templates the next generation would build on.

- Do Me — P-Square
- Personally — P-Square
- Chop My Money — P-Square
- Oliver Twist — D'banj
- Fall in Love — D'banj
- Gongo Aso — 9ice
- Bumper to Bumper — Wande Coal

## WIZKID: FROM OJUELEGBA TO ESSENCE (c. 2010-2021)

Wizkid grew from a teenage star into an international icon. 'Ojuelegba' (2014) drew a Drake remix; 'Come Closer' paired him with Drake again; and 2020's 'Essence' (with Tems) became the first Nigerian song in the Billboard Hot 100 Top 10 and the 'song of the summer'. Cross-link: his feature on Drake's 'One Dance' (2016) was a global No. 1. (Guests Tems and Burna Boy are in-region; noted.)

- Holla at Your Boy — Wizkid
- Ojuelegba — Wizkid
- Come Closer — Wizkid
- Joro — Wizkid
- Essence — Wizkid
- Ginger — Wizkid

## DAVIDO & THE 30 BILLION EMPIRE (c. 2011-2020)

Wizkid's great rival Davido brought maximalist, brass-bright anthems and an outsized persona. From the breakout 'Dami Duro' through 'Aye', 'If', and 'Fall' -- a sleeper hit that charted for years in the US -- he made big, communal, celebratory Afrobeats a stadium language.

- Dami Duro — Davido
- Aye — Davido
- If — Davido
- Fall — Davido
- Fem — Davido

## BURNA BOY & THE GRAMMY ERA (c. 2012-present)

Burna Boy fused Afrobeats with dancehall, reggae, and his self-styled 'Afro-fusion' into the genre's most critically decorated career: 'African Giant', then 'Twice as Tall' (2021 Grammy for Best Global Music Album). Cross-link and full-circle: Burna is the grandson of Fela's manager Benson Idonije and samples Fela directly -- Installment 5 made audible in the present. 'Ye' (2018) went viral partly by sharing a title with Kanye West's album.

- Ye — Burna Boy
- On the Low — Burna Boy
- Anybody — Burna Boy
- Gbona — Burna Boy
- Kilometre — Burna Boy
- Last Last — Burna Boy

## THE NEW GENERATION & THE GLOBAL CHART (c. 2019-present)

A younger wave made Afrobeats a permanent fixture of the world charts. CKay's 'Love Nwantiti' became a global TikTok phenomenon; Rema's 'Calm Down' (with a Selena Gomez remix) was one of the biggest songs of 2022-23; and Tems, Ayra Starr, Asake, and the veterans Tiwa Savage and Yemi Alade pushed the range from log-drum street-pop to global R&B. Cross-link: Tems co-wrote and sang on Beyonce's 'Move' and Future/Drake's 'Wait for U'.

- Calm Down — Rema
- Peru — Fireboy DML
- Rush — Ayra Starr
- Sungba — Asake
- Free Mind — Tems
- Johnny — Yemi Alade
- Koroba — Tiwa Savage

## GHANA: HIPLIFE, AZONTO & THE GHANAIAN WAVE (c. 2000-present)

Ghana ran its own parallel revolution. Reggie Rockstone fathered HIPLIFE (hip-hop in Twi) in the mid-1990s; the AZONTO dance craze went global around 2012; and Sarkodie, Shatta Wale, Stonebwoy, King Promise, and Gyakie carried modern Ghanaian pop worldwide. (Scope notes: 'Already' features Beyonce, from her 2019 'The Lion King: The Gift'; Fuse ODG is British-Ghanaian -- both kept with the Ghanaian lead credited and flagged.)

- Already — Shatta Wale

- Tonight — R2Bees
- Azonto — Fuse ODG
