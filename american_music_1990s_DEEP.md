# A Survey of American Music — The 1990s, Strand by Strand

The decade alternative rock became the mainstream (grunge) and hip-hop hit its songwriting
peak while splitting coast-to-coast. Two throughlines: (1) hip-hop's golden age -- East Coast
lyricism (Wu-Tang, Nas, Biggie) vs. West Coast G-funk (Dre, Snoop, 2Pac), the rivalry that
produced the deepest rap of all time; and (2) rock's pendulum swinging hard from 80s gloss to
raw, confessional grunge and then sprawling 'alternative.' Underneath, R&B modernizes (new jack
swing) then reaches back for warmth (neo-soul), and country has its biggest commercial boom ever.

## GRUNGE & THE ALT-ROCK REVOLUTION (Seattle)

In 1991 Nirvana's 'Smells Like Teen Spirit' knocked hair metal off the charts overnight and made alternative rock the mainstream. Grunge fused punk's rawness, metal's heaviness, and confessional angst — a generational reset that opened the door for every 'alternative' band of the decade.

- Come as You Are — Nirvana
- Heart-Shaped Box — Nirvana
- Alive — Pearl Jam
- Jeremy — Pearl Jam
- Even Flow — Pearl Jam
- Black Hole Sun — Soundgarden
- Man in the Box — Alice in Chains
- Would? — Alice in Chains
- Plush — Stone Temple Pilots

## ALTERNATIVE NATION: THE POST-GRUNGE SPRAWL

With 'alternative' now the center, a huge range of guitar bands flooded radio and MTV — punky, arty, jangly, ironic. This is the diverse mainstream-indie rock that defined 90s youth culture.

## EAST COAST HIP-HOP'S GOLDEN AGE (NYC)

New York rap reached a creative peak: dense lyricism, jazz and soul sampling, and cinematic street storytelling. Wu-Tang built a sprawling empire, Nas and Biggie raised the bar for narrative craft, and this is widely considered the deepest songwriting era in hip-hop.

- C.R.E.A.M. — Wu-Tang Clan
- Protect Ya Neck — Wu-Tang Clan
- N.Y. State of Mind — Nas
- The World Is Yours — Nas
- Juicy — The Notorious B.I.G.
- Big Poppa — The Notorious B.I.G.
- Hypnotize — The Notorious B.I.G.
- Shook Ones, Pt. II — Mobb Deep
- They Reminisce Over You (T.R.O.Y.) — Pete Rock & C.L. Smooth
- Mass Appeal — Gang Starr

## WEST COAST & G-FUNK (Los Angeles)

Dr. Dre took N.W.A's gangsta rap and gave it a smooth, funk-sampled sound (G-funk) built on Parliament grooves and lazy synth whines. Snoop Dogg and 2Pac became superstars, and the West Coast dominated pop radio in the mid-90s.

- Nuthin' but a 'G' Thang — Dr. Dre
- Let Me Ride — Dr. Dre
- Gin and Juice — Snoop Dogg
- California Love — 2Pac
- Dear Mama — 2Pac
- Keep Ya Head Up — 2Pac
- Regulate — Warren G and Nate Dogg
- It Was a Good Day — Ice Cube

## HIP-HOP EXPANDS: SOUTH, MIDWEST & JAZZ RAP

Rap spread beyond the coasts and diversified. OutKast made Atlanta a hip-hop capital; the jazz-inflected, Afrocentric Native Tongues movement offered a bohemian alternative; and the roots of the Southern sound that would dominate the 2000s took hold.

- Players Ball — OutKast
- Elevators (Me & You) — OutKast
- Scenario — A Tribe Called Quest
- Can I Kick It? — A Tribe Called Quest
- Award Tour — A Tribe Called Quest
- Mr. Wendal — Arrested Development
- Tennessee — Arrested Development
- Insane in the Brain — Cypress Hill

## NEW JACK SWING & 90s R&B

Teddy Riley fused R&B melody with hip-hop's swung, hard-hitting beats (new jack swing), modernizing soul for the hip-hop generation. Vocal groups and powerhouse singers ruled the charts, bridging Motown tradition and the rap era.

- Poison — Bell Biv DeVoe
- Motownphilly — Boyz II Men
- End of the Road — Boyz II Men
- No Diggity — Blackstreet
- My Lovin' (You're Never Gonna Get It) — En Vogue
- Waterfalls — TLC
- No Scrubs — TLC
- Creep — TLC
- I Will Always Love You — Whitney Houston
- Weak — SWV

## NEO-SOUL & THE HIP-HOP SOUL DIVAS

A back-to-roots R&B movement blended classic soul warmth with hip-hop sensibility and live instrumentation. D'Angelo, Erykah Badu, and Lauryn Hill led a self-consciously artful, organic counter to the slick mainstream.

- Brown Sugar — D'Angelo
- On & On — Erykah Badu
- Doo Wop (That Thing) — Lauryn Hill
- Ex-Factor — Lauryn Hill
- Real Love — Mary J. Blige
- Be Happy — Mary J. Blige
- You Make Me Wanna... — Usher
- Un-Break My Heart — Toni Braxton

## TEEN POP & THE LATE-90s POP MACHINE

The decade closed with a manufactured-pop explosion: boy bands and teen-pop stars, largely Orlando/Sweden-produced, dominated the charts and MTV's TRL, setting up the 2000s pop landscape.

- Genie in a Bottle — Christina Aguilera
- I Want It That Way — Backstreet Boys
- Bye Bye Bye — NSYNC
- MMMBop — Hanson

- Livin' la Vida Loca — Ricky Martin

## COUNTRY'S BIGGEST BOOM

Garth Brooks brought stadium-rock showmanship to country and became one of the best-selling artists in American history. The 'hot country' boom made Nashville a pop powerhouse and launched Shania Twain's crossover era and a wave of new stars.

- Friends in Low Places — Garth Brooks
- The Dance — Garth Brooks
- Man! I Feel Like a Woman! — Shania Twain
- Any Man of Mine — Shania Twain
- Achy Breaky Heart — Billy Ray Cyrus
- Chattahoochee — Alan Jackson
- Wide Open Spaces — The Chicks
- Strawberry Wine — Deana Carter

## ELECTRONIC, INDUSTRIAL & NU-METAL SEEDS

Electronic dance music crossed into US alt-culture, industrial rock brought machine aggression to the mainstream (Nine Inch Nails), and the fusion of metal with hip-hop began — nu-metal and rap-rock that would explode around 2000.
