# The Story of European Music — Installment 8: European Pop & Rock, 1950s-1970s

The pop/rock explosion. Throughlines: (1) postwar national pop (chanson, San Remo, schlager)
before rock; (2) the BRITISH INVASION -- UK kids absorbing American rock/blues and firing it
back to conquer the world, the biggest event in European pop; (3) that ambition growing into
art-rock and PROG (rock meets classical scale); and -- most consequentially -- (4) Germany's
KRAUTROCK and KRAFTWERK inventing electronic pop, the seed of disco, synth-pop, hip-hop,
techno, and house. Plus ABBA perfecting the pop single via Eurovision, and folk-rock plugging
in the ancient ballads from Installment 1. Note: a few UK-broke acts (Hendrix) are American.

## POSTWAR POP BEFORE ROCK: CHANSON, SCHLAGER & SAN REMO (c. 1950-1962)

Before rock arrived, European pop meant national traditions of sophisticated song: the French chanson (poetic, literary), Italian melodic pop crystallized by the San Remo Festival, and German/Nordic 'schlager' (sentimental easy-listening). This was mainstream European pop, sung in national languages, and it never fully went away.

- Ne me quitte pas — Jacques Brel
- La Boheme — Charles Aznavour
- Que reste-t-il de nos amours — Charles Trenet
- Nel blu dipinto di blu (Volare) — Domenico Modugno
- Tintarella di luna — Mina
- Les Feuilles mortes (Autumn Leaves) — Yves Montand

## THE BEAT BOOM & BRITISH INVASION (UK, c. 1962-1966)

American rock and roll and blues crossed the Atlantic, and British kids -- raised on skiffle -- studied it obsessively and fired it back, conquering the world. The Beatles rewrote what a pop group could be; the Stones and others sold America its own blues. This is the single biggest event in European pop, and it reset global music.

- I Want to Hold Your Hand — The Beatles
- A Hard Day's Night — The Beatles
- Yesterday — The Beatles
- (I Can't Get No) Satisfaction — The Rolling Stones
- Paint It Black — The Rolling Stones
- You Really Got Me — The Kinks
- My Generation — The Who
- House of the Rising Sun — The Animals
- For Your Love — The Yardbirds

## PSYCHEDELIA, ART ROCK & THE ALBUM ERA (UK, c. 1966-1971)

British rock grew ambitious and experimental -- the studio as instrument, concept albums, blues-rock virtuosity. The Beatles' late work, Pink Floyd's space-rock, Hendrix (American, but he broke in London), and the birth of hard rock and heavy metal reshaped what rock could be.

- A Day in the Life — The Beatles
- Strawberry Fields Forever — The Beatles
- Whole Lotta Love — Led Zeppelin
- Stairway to Heaven — Led Zeppelin
- See Emily Play — Pink Floyd
- Sunshine of Your Love — Cream
- Paranoid — Black Sabbath
- All Along the Watchtower — Jimi Hendrix

## PROGRESSIVE ROCK: EUROPE'S ART-ROCK AMBITION (UK/Europe, c. 1969-1977)

Europe fused rock with classical scale, jazz complexity, and conceptual ambition into prog -- long-form, virtuosic, and unafraid of the epic. A distinctly European high-art take on rock, huge in the 70s.

- 21st Century Schizoid Man — King Crimson
- Roundabout — Yes
- Money — Pink Floyd
- Shine On You Crazy Diamond — Pink Floyd
- Bohemian Rhapsody — Queen
- Tarkus — Emerson, Lake & Palmer
- Firth of Fifth — Genesis

## KRAUTROCK & THE ELECTRONIC REVOLUTION (Germany, c. 1970-1978)

The most future-facing music in the world came from Germany. 'Krautrock' bands built hypnotic, motorik grooves, and above all KRAFTWERK invented electronic pop -- man-machine music built from synths and drum machines that would seed disco, synth-pop, hip-hop, techno, and house. Arguably the most influential European pop of the postwar era.

- Autobahn — Kraftwerk
- Trans-Europe Express — Kraftwerk
- The Robots — Kraftwerk
- Hallogallo — Neu!
- Mother Sky — Can
- Stratosfear — Tangerine Dream
- Oxygene, Pt. 4 — Jean-Michel Jarre

## EUROVISION, EURO-POP & THE CONTINENTAL CHART (c. 1965-1979)

Continental Europe built its own glossy international pop -- much of it launched or amplified by the Eurovision Song Contest, which turned pan-European pop into a shared spectacle. ABBA emerged from it to become the biggest European pop act of the decade and master the perfect pop record.

- Waterloo — ABBA
- Dancing Queen — ABBA
- Mamma Mia — ABBA
- SOS — ABBA
- Je t'aime... moi non plus — Serge Gainsbourg and Jane Birkin
- Poupee de cire, poupee de son — France Gall
- Daddy Cool — Boney M.
- Love Is Blue (L'amour est bleu) — Paul Mauriat

## FOLK REVIVAL & FOLK-ROCK (c. 1965-1978)

A parallel back-to-roots movement plugged in traditional music: the British folk revival and electric folk-rock reworked centuries-old ballads (some you heard in Installment 1) with rock instruments, and Celtic and continental folk found new audiences.

- Matty Groves — Fairport Convention
- All Around My Hat — Steeleye Span
- The Battle of Evermore — Led Zeppelin
- Scarborough Fair / Canticle — (Traditional)
- Whiskey in the Jar — Thin Lizzy
