# The Story of Middle Eastern & North African Music — Installment 4: Turkish Music — Ottoman Classical, Arabesk, Anatolian Rock & Pop

Turkey sits at the crossroads of the maqam world, and its music blends Ottoman refinement, Anatolian folk soul, and a restless modernity. Ottoman classical music (Turk sanat muzigi) is built on the MAKAM system and the fasil suite, and its most sublime form is the MEVLEVI Sufi music of the whirling dervishes, where the reed flute laments Rumi's longing for the divine. Beneath it runs a vast folk tradition of the saz and the ASIK bards (Asik Veysel). The 20th century added the gazino-nightclub art music of icons like Zeki Muren; the fatalistic urban ARABESK of Orhan Gencebay and Ibrahim Tatlises; a fierce ANATOLIAN ROCK that fused folk and psychedelia (Baris Manco, Cem Karaca, Selda Bagcan); and a modern pop that went global through Sezen Aksu and Tarkan's worldwide 'Simarik'. Cross-links: the makam-maqam-dastgah family (#1, #3); Rumi and the Sufi world; Greek rebetiko and the Balkans (Europe survey); the Turkish-German diaspora. Content notes: the post-1980-coup persecution of musicians and arabesk's long stigma are kept factual and with care. Names transliterated (Turkish diacritics simplified to ASCII). Region: Turkey.

## OTTOMAN CLASSICAL: THE MAKAM & THE FASIL

Ottoman art music refined the makam into an elaborate courtly system, with its own comma-based tuning, rhythmic cycles (usul), and a suite form, the FASIL. Composers like Dede Efendi and the 17th-century Itri built its repertoire, and the tanbur-and-kemence virtuoso Tanburi Cemil Bey left dazzling early recordings around 1910.

## THE MEVLEVI & SUFI MUSIC

The most transcendent Ottoman music is sacred: the MEVLEVI ceremony of the whirling dervishes, the order of Rumi (Mevlana) in Konya. In the sema ritual, dancers spin toward the divine while the ney reed-flute sings a lament of longing -- Rumi's 'listen to the reed' made sound. Cross-link: Rumi, shared with the Persian world.

## TURKISH FOLK & THE ASIK BARDS

Anatolia's folk music (halk muzigi) is carried by the saz (baglama), a long-necked lute that is the soul of Turkey, and by the ASIK -- wandering bard-poets. The blind Asik Veysel sang immortal songs of earth and mortality ('Uzun Ince Bir Yoldayim', 'Kara Toprak'); Neset Ertas and the Alevi deyis tradition carry it on.

## THE GAZINO VOICES: ZEKI MUREN & ART MUSIC

In the mid-century gazino nightclubs, Turkish art music (sanat muzigi) produced flamboyant superstars. Zeki Muren -- the 'Sun of Art', a beloved and boundary-breaking icon -- reigned with songs like 'Simdi Uzaklardasin', beside Muzeyyen Senar and, later, Bulent Ersoy. Refined, romantic, and theatrical, it was Turkey's classical pop.

## ARABESK: THE MELANCHOLY OF THE CITY

ARABESK gave voice to the millions who migrated to the cities: a fatalistic, string-drenched, Arab-and-Egyptian-inflected pop of heartbreak and hard fate. Orhan Gencebay founded it ('Batsin Bu Dunya', 'Bir Teselli Ver'), and Ibrahim Tatlises and Muslum Gurses became its idols. Content note: state media long looked down on and restricted arabesk.

## ANATOLIAN ROCK (c. 1968-1980)

Anadolu rock fused the saz and Turkish folk melody with psychedelic rock into one of the world's great regional rock sounds. Baris Manco ('Daglar Daglar') was its beloved showman, Cem Karaca ('Namus Belasi') its firebrand, Erkin Koray ('Cemalim') its psych pioneer, and Selda Bagcan its protest voice. Content note: several were persecuted after the 1980 coup.

- Daglar Daglar (Mountains, Mountains) — Baris Manco
- Cemalim — Erkin Koray

## MODERN TURKISH POP (c. 1975-present)

Turkish pop conquered its own and the world's charts. Sezen Aksu -- the 'Queen of Turkish Pop' and songwriter-mentor to a generation ('Firuze') -- shaped the modern sound; Tarkan took it global with 'Simarik' (Kiss Kiss); and Sertab Erener won Eurovision in 2003. Turkish pop became a regional powerhouse.
