# The Story of European Music — Installment 9: European Pop, 1980s-Present (bringing it home)

The final stretch, and a satisfying reversal: for most of this survey Europe absorbed American
forms; now Europe EXPORTS the world's pop. Throughlines: (1) SYNTH-POP -- Kraftwerk's blueprint
becomes the 80s mainstream (Depeche Mode, New Order); (2) the RAVE explosion -- Europe takes
American house/techno and turns it into a mass movement, then re-exports it; (3) BRITPOP's guitar
revival; (4) the FRENCH TOUCH and trip-hop making electronic music high art; and (5) the modern
era where Swedish production and European EDM DJs supply the global pop machine -- including the
exact acts pulled from your American 2010s file. The transatlantic loop, closed.

## SYNTH-POP & NEW ROMANTICS (UK/Europe, c. 1979-1985)

Kraftwerk's blueprint plus cheap synthesizers birthed synth-pop -- sleek, electronic, emotional pop that dominated the early-80s charts and defined the MTV-era European sound. The New Romantics added glamour and art-school theatricality.

- Enola Gay — Orchestral Manoeuvres in the Dark
- Just Can't Get Enough — Depeche Mode
- Enjoy the Silence — Depeche Mode
- Don't You Want Me — The Human League
- Tainted Love — Soft Cell
- Vienna — Ultravox
- Sweet Dreams (Are Made of This) — Eurythmics
- Blue Monday — New Order
- Cars — Gary Numan

## THE 80s CHART: POST-PUNK, POP & STADIUM ROCK (Europe, c. 1980-1989)

Beyond the synths, European pop and rock ran the gamut -- arty post-punk, jangly guitar pop, and huge stadium acts. Britain and Ireland especially produced globe-conquering bands, while continental pop and 'schlager' kept their own audiences.

- Love Will Tear Us Apart — Joy Division
- This Charming Man — The Smiths
- With or Without You — U2
- Where the Streets Have No Name — U2
- Every Breath You Take — The Police
- Under Pressure — Queen and David Bowie
- Heroes — David Bowie
- 99 Luftballons — Nena
- Take On Me — a-ha

## ACID HOUSE, TECHNO & THE RAVE EXPLOSION (c. 1987-1995)

The 'Second Summer of Love.' American house and techno (from Chicago and Detroit) landed in Europe and detonated into a mass youth movement -- acid house, warehouse raves, and Ecstasy culture. Europe took the underground American sound and turned it into a continent-wide phenomenon, then re-exported it.

- Voodoo Ray — A Guy Called Gerald
- Pacific State — 808 State
- Papua New Guinea — Future Sound of London
- Born Slippy (Nuxx) — Underworld
- Chime — Orbital
- Little Fluffy Clouds — The Orb
- Windowlicker — Aphex Twin
- Charly — The Prodigy

## EURODANCE & CONTINENTAL POP (c. 1989-1999)

Continental Europe built a glossy, euphoric, hi-NRG dance-pop -- big diva vocals, rap verses, and pounding four-on-the-floor beats -- that ruled clubs and charts across the continent (and often the US). Cheesy, joyful, and enormously popular.

- Rhythm Is a Dancer — Snap!
- What Is Love — Haddaway
- The Rhythm of the Night — Corona
- Blue (Da Ba Dee) — Eiffel 65
- Barbie Girl — Aqua
- Around the World — Daft Punk
- Better Off Alone — Alice Deejay

## BRITPOP & THE GUITAR REVIVAL (UK, c. 1994-1999)

A self-consciously British guitar-pop movement reacting against American grunge -- witty, melodic, and steeped in the Beatles/Kinks tradition. Oasis vs. Blur became a national event, and Britpop briefly made guitar bands the center of pop again.

- Wonderwall — Oasis
- Don't Look Back in Anger — Oasis
- Common People — Pulp
- Parklife — Blur
- Song 2 — Blur
- Bitter Sweet Symphony — The Verve
- Karma Police — Radiohead

## FRENCH TOUCH, TRIP-HOP & THE ELECTRONIC MAINSTREAM (c. 1995-2010)

European electronic music matured into sophisticated art and global pop. France's 'French touch' (Daft Punk, Air) filtered disco through house; Britain's trip-hop (Massive Attack, Portishead) made moody, cinematic beats; and the sound went fully mainstream worldwide.

- One More Time — Daft Punk
- Digital Love — Daft Punk
- Get Lucky — Daft Punk
- Sexy Boy — Air
- Unfinished Sympathy — Massive Attack
- Teardrop — Massive Attack
- Glory Box — Portishead
- Right Here, Right Now — Fatboy Slim

## THE MODERN ERA: EDM, GLOBAL POP & EURO-EXPORTS (c. 2008-present)

Europe now supplies much of the world's pop machinery -- Swedish songwriting/production dominates the global charts, and European DJs built the EDM boom that took over American radio (these are the acts pulled from the US survey; they live here). Plus a new generation of European pop and rap stars.
