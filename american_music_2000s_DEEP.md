# A Survey of American Music — The 2000s, Strand by Strand

The decade hip-hop became the undisputed center of pop culture and the internet blew up the
music business. Throughlines: (1) rap goes from genre to dominant force -- Jay-Z/Eminem as the
biggest stars, then Kanye reinvents the album, then the SOUTH takes over (crunk/snap) and plants
the seeds of trap; (2) a guitar backlash -- the garage/indie revival (Strokes, White Stripes)
growing into the blog-era indie sprawl; and (3) R&B's sleek, futuristic golden run. The industry
shifts from CDs to Napster to iTunes to the first streaming -- singles and ringtones over albums.

## RAP BECOMES THE DOMINANT POP MUSIC

The 2000s is when hip-hop stopped being a genre and became the center of pop culture. Jay-Z and Eminem became the biggest stars in music; the mogul era (Roc-A-Fella, Bad Boy, Shady/Aftermath) turned rappers into CEOs. Rap now set the agenda for everything else.

## KANYE & THE PRODUCER-AUTEUR SHIFT

Kanye West moved rap from street narratives toward soul-sampling introspection and then art-pop ambition, redefining what a rap album could be. Producers (Timbaland, The Neptunes) became stars in their own right, and their futuristic beats reshaped all of pop.

## THE SOUTH TAKES OVER (crunk, snap & Atlanta trap roots)

Southern rap became the commercial engine of the decade. Atlanta crunk brought rowdy party energy, snap music simplified it for ringtones and clubs, and pioneers like T.I. and Young Jeezy laid the foundations of trap — the sound that would rule the 2010s.

## R&B'S SLEEK 2000s GOLDEN RUN

R&B fused fully with hip-hop production and became glossy, futuristic, and chart-dominating. Beyonce went solo and superstar, Usher and Alicia Keys defined the sound, and the Neptunes/Timbaland productions made it the most forward-looking pop of the decade.

## GARAGE & INDIE ROCK REVIVAL

A back-to-basics guitar rebellion against nu-metal and pop. The Strokes and White Stripes led a stripped-down garage-rock revival out of New York and Detroit, kicking off the indie-rock decade and the blog-hyped scene that followed.

- Last Nite — The Strokes
- Hard to Explain — The Strokes
- Seven Nation Army — The White Stripes

- Mr. Brightside — The Killers

- House of Jealous Lovers — The Rapture
- Evil — Interpol

## INDIE GOES BIG: THE BLOG-ERA SPRAWL

Indie rock grew ambitious and emotional — orchestral, anthemic, and increasingly popular. Arcade Fire and their peers proved 'indie' could fill arenas, and the internet (blogs, then streaming) became the new tastemaker.

## POP: TEEN IDOLS TO DANCE-POP

Pop moved from turn-of-the-decade teen idols toward a slicker, more adult dance-pop, and by 2008-09 the electro-pop wave (Lady Gaga) that would define the next era arrived. Timberlake took the boy-band graduate to credible solo superstardom.
