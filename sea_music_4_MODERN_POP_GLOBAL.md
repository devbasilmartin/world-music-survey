# The Story of Southeast Asian Music — Installment 4 (Finale): Modern Pop & the Global Present (Dangdut, T-Pop, V-Pop, P-Pop, Hip-Hop & Indie)

The final Southeast Asian chapter carries the region from the crooner age into a booming, globally-connected present. Indonesia's DANGDUT -- the swaying, Indian-and-Malay-rooted people's music of Rhoma Irama and Elvy Sukaesih -- became the sound of the archipelago and mutated into the high-energy dangdut KOPLO of Via Vallen and Nella Kharisma. Thailand fused string pop, the conscience-rock of CARABAO's 'songs for life', and superstar T-POP (Thongchai 'Bird' McIntyre, and today 4EVE and BUS). Vietnam's V-POP produced the megastar Son Tung M-TP and the diva My Tam; the Philippines' band boom (the Eraserheads) grew into a world-class P-POP wave (SB19, the folk-pop of Ben&Ben); and Malaysia's Siti Nurhaliza reigned as a regional queen. Meanwhile SE Asian HIP-HOP and INDIE went global -- Indonesia's Rich Brian and the 88rising movement, Thai and Indonesian indie -- carrying the region's sound to the world. This closes the Southeast Asia survey. Content notes: Carabao's protest songs and the region's political music are kept factual and with care. Names transliterated. Cross-links: the crooner era (#3); mainland & island classical (#1-2); K-pop & J-pop of the East Asia surveys. Region: present-day Southeast Asia.

## INDONESIA: DANGDUT, THE PEOPLE'S MUSIC

Dangdut -- named for the 'dang-dut' of its drum -- fused Indian film music, Malay orkes melayu, and rock into Indonesia's most popular sound, the music of the streets and the masses. Rhoma Irama, the 'King of Dangdut', turned it into a vehicle for message and spectacle; Elvy Sukaesih reigned as its queen.

## INDONESIA: KOPLO & THE MODERN DANGDUT WAVE

In the 2000s, dangdut KOPLO -- a faster, drum-driven East Javanese street style -- exploded, and a new generation of stars took it viral. Via Vallen and Nella Kharisma became national sensations, and 'Meraih Bintang' carried dangdut-pop to the 2018 Asian Games. Modern Indonesian pop-rock (Noah/Peterpan) filled the stadiums alongside it.

## THAILAND: STRING POP & SONGS FOR LIFE

Thai popular music split into commercial STRING pop and the conscience-driven PHLENG PHUEA CHIWIT ('songs for life'). Carabao's anthemic 'Made in Thailand' turned folk-rock into social commentary; the Grammy-era string pop crowned Thongchai 'Bird' McIntyre the enduring King of Thai Pop. Content note: songs-for-life carried political weight, kept factual.

## VIETNAM: V-POP

Vietnamese pop -- V-POP -- became a powerhouse of the 2010s. Son Tung M-TP fused R&B, EDM, and Vietnamese melody into record-shattering hits ('Hay Trao Cho Anh'); the beloved diva My Tam reigned for two decades; and viral hits like 'Hai Phut Hon' carried V-pop to the world.

## THE PHILIPPINES: THE BAND BOOM & P-POP

The Philippines' 1990s band explosion -- led by the Eraserheads, the 'Beatles of the Philippines' ('Ang Huling El Bimbo') -- remade OPM. From it grew a world-class present: the folk-pop of Ben&Ben, the R&B of Moira dela Torre, and the globally-charting P-POP idol group SB19, the first Filipino act to break internationally.

## MALAYSIA, SINGAPORE & THE MALAY POP WORLD
