# The Story of Latin American Music — Installment 7: Contemporary / Global (c. 1995-present)

The story closes where it now leads the planet. In the last thirty years Latin American music stops being a regional export and becomes the center of global pop. REGGAETON is born in Panama and Puerto Rico from Jamaican dancehall and the dembow riddim, and by the 2010s -- through 'Despacito', J Balvin, Karol G, and Bad Bunny -- Spanish-language music tops charts everywhere on Earth for the first time. Along the way the 1990s 'Latin explosion' (Shakira, Ricky Martin, Juanes) crosses over; Brazil grows AXE, PAGODE, funk carioca, and stadium SERTANEJO; ROCK EN ESPANOL matures into a Latin-alternative art music (Cafe Tacvba, Cerati); a new folk generation (Jorge Drexler, Natalia Lafourcade, Lila Downs, Susana Baca) carries nueva cancion's conscience forward; NEO-TANGO and a bossa revival keep the instrumental tradition alive; and composers like Arturo Marquez and Osvaldo Golijov fill the world's concert halls. Cross-links to the US survey are now total -- reggaeton and Latin trap ARE mainstream US pop. Puerto Rican, Panamanian, and Dominican artists appear throughout as pan-Latin; Spanish artists (Enrique Iglesias, Rosalia) are cut as European. Scope calls are flagged, not buried. Content note: this is also music of migration, the drug war, and the barrio -- kept with its context.

## REGGAETON IS BORN: PANAMA, PUERTO RICO & THE DEMBOW (c. 1990-2005)

The most consequential new genre in decades began when Jamaican dancehall met Spanish in Panama's 'reggae en espanol' (El General) and was reforged in the Puerto Rican underground over the dembow riddim. Vico C brought hip-hop, and in 2004 Daddy Yankee's 'Gasolina' broke it worldwide, with Tego Calderon and Ivy Queen giving it depth and a woman's voice. (Scope call: this is Panamanian and Puerto Rican music, Caribbean and pan-Latin at once -- kept here as the continent's dominant new sound, and shared with the US and open Caribbean surveys; flagged. Cross-link: the dembow traces straight to Jamaica.)

- La Recta Final — Vico C
- Gasolina — Daddy Yankee
- Loiza — Tego Calderon
- Yo Quiero Bailar — Ivy Queen
- Rakata — Wisin y Yandel

## THE LATIN EXPLOSION: CROSSOVER POP GOES GLOBAL (c. 1995-2005)

For a few years around 2000, Latin pop crossed fully into the Anglo mainstream. Ricky Martin's 'Livin' la Vida Loca' kicked open the door; Colombia's Shakira and Juanes conquered the world in Spanish and English; and Carlos Vives fused vallenato with pop-rock. (Scope call: the Spanish-born Enrique Iglesias, central to this moment commercially, is cut as European -- the region's own stars carry the thread.)

## BRAZIL: AXE, PAGODE & THE SAMBA REVIVAL (c. 1992-2008)

Bahia's carnival blocos powered AXE music (Daniela Mercury, the Afro-bloco Olodum) into a national craze, while the romantic PAGODE of Raca Negra and the party-samba of Grupo E o Tchan filled the pop charts. In counterpoint, Zeca Pagodinho led a roots-samba revival that reasserted the genre's Rio soul. Cross-link: Olodum's drumming reached the world via Paul Simon and Michael Jackson.

- Farao — Olodum

- Deixa Acontecer — Raca Negra
- Deixa a Vida Me Levar — Zeca Pagodinho
- Camarao que Dorme a Onda Leva — Zeca Pagodinho

## FUNK CARIOCA, SERTANEJO & THE BRAZILIAN POP MAINSTREAM (c. 2000-present)

Two working-class musics became Brazil's mainstream: FUNK CARIOCA, born in Rio's favela baile parties over the Miami-bass 'tamborzao' beat, and SERTANEJO UNIVERSITARIO, the country-pop of the interior that produced a global novelty smash in 'Ai Se Eu Te Pego'. Anitta then fused funk with global pop to become Brazil's biggest modern export. Content note: funk carioca is often criminalized and censored as favela music -- kept with that context.

- Ai Se Eu Te Pego — Michel Telo
- Evidencias — Chitaozinho e Xororo
- Show das Poderosas — Anitta
- Baile de Favela — MC Joao
- Meteoro — Luan Santana

## LATIN ALTERNATIVE: ROCK EN ESPAÑOL BECOMES ART (c. 1992-2010)

The rock of Installment 6 matured into a self-aware LATIN ALTERNATIVE avant-garde. Mexico's Cafe Tacvba dismantled and rebuilt every Latin genre; Molotov brought rap-metal protest; Colombia's Aterciopelados and Mexico's Julieta Venegas rewired pop; and Gustavo Cerati's solo work made him the elder statesman of the whole continent's rock. Cross-link: this scene built the 'Latin Alternative' Grammy category and toured the same US circuit as indie rock.

## NEW FOLK & THE HEIRS OF NUEVA CANCION (c. 1995-present)

Nueva cancion's conscience passed to a new generation of songwriters. Uruguay's Jorge Drexler won an Oscar for a whispered folk song; Mexico's Natalia Lafourcade and Lila Downs recovered ranchera and Indigenous roots as high art; Peru's Susana Baca carried the Afro-Peruvian revival begun by Nicomedes Santa Cruz (Installment 4) to a Grammy and a cabinet post; and Chavela Vargas closed a legendary career singing the ranchera as raw confession. Living tradition and Afro-descendant memory stay central.

- Nunca Es Suficiente — Natalia Lafourcade

- Maria Lando — Susana Baca
- Paloma Negra — Chavela Vargas

## NEO-TANGO, THE BOSSA REVIVAL & THE INSTRUMENTAL PRESENT (c. 1998-present)

Piazzolla's heirs plugged the tango into electronica: Gotan Project and Bajofondo built NEO-TANGO for global dancefloors, while Gustavo Santaolalla's spare guitar became the sound of world cinema. In Brazil, Marisa Monte, Seu Jorge, and Bebel Gilberto carried samba and bossa into a cool, global present. (Scope call: Gotan Project is Paris-based but Argentine-led and tango to the core -- kept, and flagged.)

- Amor I Love You — Marisa Monte
- Tive Razao — Seu Jorge
- Aganju — Bebel Gilberto

## THE STREAMING ERA: LATIN MUSIC TAKES OVER THE WORLD (c. 2010-present)

Streaming erased the border. 'Despacito' became the most-watched video in history; Colombia's J Balvin, Karol G, and Maluma and Puerto Rico's Bad Bunny turned reggaeton and Latin trap into the planet's default pop, and Mexico's Peso Pluma made corridos tumbados a global phenomenon. For the first time, Spanish-language music routinely tops the worldwide charts. (Scope call: the Puerto Rican and Panamanian urbano stars are kept as pan-Latin, as throughout; the Spanish superstar Rosalia is cut as European. Cross-link: this is now indistinguishable from mainstream US pop.)

## ART MUSIC NOW: THE CONTEMPORARY CONCERT HALL (c. 1994-present)

Latin American art music sits at the heart of today's global repertoire. Arturo Marquez's 'Danzon No. 2' has become the most-played orchestral work by any living Latin American composer; Osvaldo Golijov fuses tango, klezmer, and flamenco into oratorio and opera; and Gabriela Ortiz carries Mexican modernism into the world's leading orchestras. (Scope note: the US-born Peruvian-American Gabriela Lena Frank is cut as belonging to the US survey.) Name the work, not the performer.

- Danzon No. 2 — Arturo Marquez
- Conga del Fuego Nuevo — Arturo Marquez
- La Pasion segun San Marcos — Osvaldo Golijov
- Ainadamar: excerpts — Osvaldo Golijov
- Kauyumari — Gabriela Ortiz
