# A Survey of American Music — Minstrelsy Restored (Era 3 supplement, content warning)

RESTORED FOR HISTORICAL COMPLETENESS — content warning. Blackface minstrelsy was the most popular American entertainment of the mid-19th century, and it is inseparable from the story of American music: it was the first mass channel for Black-derived banjo and rhythm into white culture, it made the first American pop stars, and its caricatures hardened into law and stereotype (the term 'Jim Crow' comes directly from a minstrel act). These titles are included because they shaped what came after, not because they're pleasant. Period-accurate minstrel recordings are scarce, so most are marked Traditional; search carefully. Several of these carry racist content in their original lyrics — included here as historical documents.
