# The Story of European Music — Installment 5: The Romantic Era, Part 2 — Nationalism & Late Romantics (c. 1860-1910)

The other half of the Romantic century. Two big throughlines: (1) NATIONALISM -- composers
across Russia, Bohemia, Scandinavia, and Spain deliberately building distinct NATIONAL voices
out of folk song and homeland identity (the Russian Five, Tchaikovsky, Dvorak, Grieg,
Sibelius); and (2) the LATE-ROMANTIC orchestra pushed to its absolute limit -- Mahler's world-
sized symphonies and Strauss's vivid tone poems stretching tonality as far as it can go, right
before the 20th century breaks it apart. Rachmaninoff carries lush Romanticism into the new era.

## RUSSIAN NATIONALISM: THE MIGHTY HANDFUL (c. 1860-1890)

A group of Russian composers ('The Five') deliberately built a national art music from Russian folk song, church chant, and Orientalist color -- rejecting German models to sound distinctly Russian. Mussorgsky's raw originality and Rimsky-Korsakov's dazzling orchestration lead the way.

- Pictures at an Exhibition: The Great Gate of Kiev — Modest Mussorgsky
- Night on Bald Mountain — Modest Mussorgsky
- Scheherazade: The Sea and Sinbad's Ship — Nikolai Rimsky-Korsakov
- Flight of the Bumblebee — Nikolai Rimsky-Korsakov
- Prince Igor: Polovtsian Dances — Alexander Borodin
- In the Steppes of Central Asia — Alexander Borodin

## TCHAIKOVSKY: RUSSIA MEETS THE WEST (1840-1893)

Tchaikovsky fused Russian melody and deep emotion with Western Classical form, becoming the most beloved Russian composer worldwide. His ballets defined the genre and his symphonies wear their heart fully exposed -- lush, dramatic, and instantly recognizable.

- Swan Lake: Scene (Swan Theme) — Pyotr Ilyich Tchaikovsky
- The Nutcracker: Dance of the Sugar Plum Fairy — Pyotr Ilyich Tchaikovsky
- 1812 Overture: Finale — Pyotr Ilyich Tchaikovsky
- Piano Concerto No. 1: Opening — Pyotr Ilyich Tchaikovsky
- Symphony No. 6 (Pathetique): Finale — Pyotr Ilyich Tchaikovsky
- Romeo and Juliet: Love Theme — Pyotr Ilyich Tchaikovsky
- The Sleeping Beauty: Waltz — Pyotr Ilyich Tchaikovsky

## CZECH, SCANDINAVIAN & OTHER NATIONAL VOICES (c. 1860-1910)

National schools bloom across Europe, each drawing on homeland folk idioms. Dvorak and Smetana give voice to Bohemia, Grieg to Norway, Sibelius to Finland (his music became a symbol of national independence), and Albeniz to Spain. Folk material becomes serious concert art.

- Symphony No. 9 (From the New World): Largo — Antonin Dvorak
- Slavonic Dance No. 8 in G minor — Antonin Dvorak
- The Moldau (Vltava) — Bedrich Smetana
- Peer Gynt: In the Hall of the Mountain King — Edvard Grieg
- Peer Gynt: Morning Mood — Edvard Grieg
- Piano Concerto in A minor: Opening — Edvard Grieg
- Finlandia — Jean Sibelius
- Asturias (Leyenda) — Isaac Albeniz

## THE LATE-ROMANTIC ORCHESTRA AT ITS LIMIT (c. 1880-1910)

The Romantic orchestra swells to enormous size and expressive extremity. Mahler makes the symphony a vast philosophical universe ('a symphony must be like the world; it must contain everything'); Richard Strauss masters the vivid tone poem; Bruckner's spiritual monoliths continue. Music is stretched about as far as tonality can go.

- Symphony No. 5: Adagietto — Gustav Mahler
- Symphony No. 1 (Titan): Finale — Gustav Mahler
- Symphony No. 2 (Resurrection): Finale — Gustav Mahler
- Also sprach Zarathustra: Introduction (Sunrise) — Richard Strauss
- Don Juan — Richard Strauss
- Der Rosenkavalier: Final Trio — Richard Strauss

## LATE-ROMANTIC PIANO, CONCERTO & OPERA (c. 1880-1910)

The grand Romantic tradition reaches a final flowering: Rachmaninoff's sweeping piano Romanticism (carried into the 20th century), Grieg and Franck's concerted works, and the rise of Italian 'verismo' opera -- gritty, passionate, real-life drama -- in Puccini and Leoncavallo.

- Piano Concerto No. 2: Opening — Sergei Rachmaninoff
- Prelude in C-sharp minor — Sergei Rachmaninoff
- Rhapsody on a Theme of Paganini: Variation 18 — Sergei Rachmaninoff
- La Boheme: Che gelida manina — Giacomo Puccini
- Turandot: Nessun dorma — Giacomo Puccini
- Gianni Schicchi: O mio babbino caro — Giacomo Puccini
- Pagliacci: Vesti la giubba — Ruggero Leoncavallo
- Symphony in D minor: Allegro non troppo — Cesar Franck
